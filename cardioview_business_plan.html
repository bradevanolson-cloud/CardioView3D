The Problem

The Current Challenge: 2D Angiography Limitations

Today's cardiac catheterization labs rely on 2D angiographic images like those shown below. While these provide valuable diagnostic information, they present significant challenges for accurate 3D assessment of coronary anatomy and stenosis severity.

Current State: Traditional 2D Angiography

Single-Angle View Limitations

Single projection — overlap & foreshortening obscure true stenosis

Challenges:
  • Vessel arangement depentant on perspective angles
  • Difficulty assessing true stenosis severity
  • Limited depth perception
  • Subjective interpretation variability

Multi-Angle Acquisition

Alternative projection — Left side coronary anatomy dependant on camera angles

Current Process:
  • Multiple 2D projections acquired
  • Cardiologist mentally reconstructs 3D anatomy mentaly
  • Time-consuming manual analysis
  • High cognitive load during procedures

DICOM Video Quality Considerations

Modern angiography systems capture high-resolution DICOM video streams at 15-30 FPS with spatial resolution of 0.2-0.3mm. However, image quality varies significantly based on:

  • Contrast injection timing and concentration
  • Patient motion and cardiac rhythm
  • C-arm positioning and angulation
  • Equipment age and calibration
  • Operator technique and experience

CardioView 3D's AI algorithms are specifically designed to handle these real-world variations and extract maximum diagnostic value from existing imaging workflows.

1. DICOM Video Ingestion & Processing

Risk Analysis & Mitigation

Comprehensive Risk Assessment Framework

Our risk analysis categorizes threats by type and prioritizes them based on likelihood and impact. Each risk includes detailed mitigation strategies informed by industry best practices for AI medtech startups in cardiac imaging.

Technical Risks

High Risk - High Impact

AI Model Accuracy Errors

Risk: AI models may produce errors in 3D rendering (e.g., missegmentation of vessels due to motion artifacts or variable anatomy), leading to inaccurate occlusion detection.

Mitigation: Implement explainable AI (XAI) techniques for model transparency; conduct rigorous validation on diverse datasets (1,000+ annotated angiography cases); use ensemble models and continuous monitoring to reduce errors to <5%; partner with cardiologists for iterative testing.

Medium Risk

API Dependency

Risk: Dependency on Grok API downtime affecting system availability.

Mitigation: Implement API redundancies (fallback to AWS ML); maintain SLAs with xAI for uptime guarantees; develop offline processing capabilities for critical functions.

Regulatory and Compliance Risks

High Risk - High Impact

FDA Approval Delays

Risk: FDA/EMA approval delays for SaMD (Software as a Medical Device) due to evolving AI guidelines, plus HIPAA/GDPR non-compliance risks in data handling.

Mitigation: Engage regulatory consultants early for 510(k) pathway; pursue FDA breakthrough designation; build HIPAA-compliant cloud infrastructure (AWS with encryption); conduct annual audits and bias assessments to meet standards like ISO 13485.

Medium Risk

Compliance Standards Evolution

Risk: Changing regulatory requirements for AI medical devices.

Mitigation: Maintain active FDA communication; build flexible compliance framework; engage with industry working groups on AI standards.

Data and Bias Risks

High Risk - Medium Impact

Dataset Bias and Limited Diversity

Risk: Limited/diverse datasets may introduce biases (e.g., underrepresentation of demographics), causing inaccurate models for non-standard patients.

Mitigation: Source balanced datasets from partnerships (e.g., MIMIC-CXR or hospital collaborations); apply debiasing algorithms during training; perform fairness audits and retrain models quarterly with new data.

Medium Risk

Data Quality and Standardization

Risk: Inconsistent DICOM data quality across institutions affecting model performance.

Mitigation: Develop robust preprocessing pipelines; establish data quality standards with hospital partners; implement automated quality checks.

Ethical and Clinical Risks

Medium Risk - High Impact

Clinical Over-Reliance

Risk: Over-reliance on AI could lead to clinician deskilling or ethical issues like data privacy breaches; potential harm from false positives/negatives in diagnostics.

Mitigation: Design as decision-support tool (not replacement); include ethical guidelines in development (e.g., ACC principles); conduct clinical trials with oversight and liability insurance; educate users via training modules.

Medium Risk

Patient Privacy and Data Security

Risk: Potential breaches of patient data during processing and storage.

Mitigation: Implement end-to-end encryption; conduct regular security audits; maintain minimal data retention policies; ensure HIPAA compliance training for all staff.

Financial and Market Risks

Medium Risk - Medium Impact

Cash Flow and Market Adoption

Risk: High burn rate from API costs or delayed revenue; market adoption slowed by competition or reimbursement issues.

Mitigation: Secure $5-10M seed with milestones; optimize Grok API usage (e.g., batch processing); pursue CPT codes for reimbursement; diversify revenue with pilots and partnerships.

Medium Risk

Competitive Response

Risk: Large players (Philips, Siemens) developing competing AI solutions.

Mitigation: Strong IP protection strategy; focus on first-mover advantage; establish exclusive hospital partnerships; continuous innovation pipeline.

Low Risk

Reimbursement Uncertainty

Risk: Delayed or limited insurance coverage for AI diagnostic tools.

Mitigation: Engage with payers early; demonstrate clear ROI; work with industry groups on CPT code development; offer value-based pricing models.

Team Retention and Scaling

Mitigation: Offer competitive equity packages and remote perks; implement strong remote collaboration tools; establish clear communication protocols; regular team building activities.
